Virus Research

Viruses are submicroscopic infectious agents that can only replicate inside the living cells of organisms. They infect all life forms, from animals and plants to microorganisms, including bacteria and archaea.

Types of Viruses

  • DNA viruses: Use DNA as their genetic material and replicate using host cell machinery.
  • RNA viruses: Use RNA as their genetic material and replicate in the cytoplasm of host cells.
  • Retroviruses: Use RNA as genetic material but convert it into DNA within a host cell (e.g., HIV).
  • Bacteriophages: Viruses that infect bacteria, often used in bacterial genetics and biotechnology.

Current Research

Research in virology focuses on understanding virus structure, replication mechanisms, and interactions with host organisms. Recent advancements include the development of antiviral drugs, vaccines, and gene therapy techniques. Studies on virus evolution and epidemiology are crucial for controlling viral outbreaks and pandemics.
